Yakshagana

Yakshagana is a traditional Indian theater form, developed in Dakshina Kannada, Udupi and Kasragod. It combines dance, music, culture, make up, stage technique with a unique style and form. In tulu language yakshagana is called as Aata.It is usually performed in dusk to dawn. Stories are drawn from Mahabharata,Ramayan,Bhagvat Gita and other epic of hindu and jain. There are 2 types of yakshagana one is tenku and other is balaga. 1st yakshagana was introduced in Udupi. In early days yakshagana would be for 12hrs but in current days it will be around 4-5hrs. In recent days female also play.

Pilivesha (Tiger Dance )

Pili yesha in Tulu Masque, is a folk dance, which is unique in coastal. It is usually performed in the month of Dasaraa or Janmashtami. During the time of dussara to honour goddess Durga, whose favoured animal is tiger,this dance is done. Young males form a troop of 5-10 or morw which will have 3-5 male painted tigers and costumes to look like tiger. There will be band set for the males to dance. This act is performed on the road, temple and some may go to individual houses. Until the last day of Navratri they perform this. The paint is painted on the naked body and will kept on the body of 2 days, and later on the same they retouched it. The painting will be using the thick layer, sometimes if may also burn the skin.

Cock fight (Kordha Katta)

It is also one the traditional aspect of Tuluadu. It is conducted in vicinity of temple or near bars and still goes on,. It is conducted at evening or night. It is a event of rural community in Mangalore. The cocks are breed with special care only for this event. To watch this event more than 100 people gather around atmosphere. The cocks are brought from Erode, Selam and Kottam Kshetra of Tamil Nadu. Mangalore city alone have 5 major shops vicinity where the cocks used in the cock fight are sold. The cock are tied with 3-4 inches sharp blade which hurts the cock when they fight, leading to the death of one cock. Entertainment and Prestige issue, there have been few criticizing the game for the amount of cruelty attached to it, initial for entertainment but later for gambling purpose. It is banned and said that it is a illegal business, but there are few who continues this inspite of issues.

The Buffalo Race (Kambala)

Around 800 yrs back, the buffalo race which is called as Kambala,was a festival in dedicated to kadri, Lord Manjunath.It is beleived that it has to be celebrated to pleaze the god for the good harvest. Anually for centuries in coastal district is tradition is followed. It is contest where 2 pairs of buffalo tied ploughs are made to race on slush-filled tracks with a jockey giuoly them while they run. Once it was said that Kambala should be banned but later the Supreme Court gave permission to celebrate this. Buffalo are not really speed runners. But no they are made to run as fast as 100mtrs in 12sec. Earlier winner of buffalo race used to be rewarded with a coconut, but in recent days medals, cash and trophies are given.

Bhoota Kola

Bhoota Kola is a ritual folk dance from South Canara. Literal translation of the word Bhoota would be ghost. Mostly in Mangalore, it means a holy spirit, often of common men who have elevated themselves to this position through a heroic act and are believed to have gained Godly powers after death. Some holy spirits also draw references to animals and others are believed to be Ishwara Ganas (Representatives of lord Shiva). There are three types of holy spirits - The one at home, for the welfare of the family. The one that safeguards the land and the one that protects a village (Grama). Bhoota Kola is a way of offering food to the holy spirits which involves a Pambada (One who dresses as the holy spirit) becoming possessed and acts as an oracle for the deity being channelised.
